if you already know what toki pona is and a few background details about it, you can likely skip this lesson and continue onto the next one :)

toki pona is a constructed language (like others, such as Esperanto, Hebrew, and Interslavic) - however, unlike most, its goal is not to aid in communication, its goal is far more philosophical.
its biggest selling point is the word count - 120-150, depending on who you ask; but more frequently lands on 137, nowadays. however, please dont let this fool you; while the biggest challenge in language learning is indeed memorising vocabulary, toki pona's philosophy makes learning the language much harder than appears.
we will go over this philosophy in the second chapter; usefully, titled "is toki pona for you?"!

the language was first made in 2001, and properly "published" by Sonja Lang in 2014, with the release of the first toki pona book "Toki Pona: The Language of Good", which is often referred to as "pu". Since, another book has been released (in 2021), titled "Toki Pona Dictionary", called "ku" in the community.

toki pona derives its root words from English, Tok Pisin, Finnish, Georgian, Dutch, Acadian French, Esperanto, Serbo-Croatian, and Chinese, with the following languages only having one derived from: Welsh, Tongan, Akan, and Swahili.
the language has often been compared to a pidgin language; its minimalist nature, and focus on the core aspects of communication as well as limited phonology, help it achieve this sort of feel!

i personally find the language absolutely fascinating, and the community amazing; and i hope you feel the same way after this course! :)
